Despite rapid advances in large language
model (LLM) capabilities, it remains unclear
whether progress in educational NLP trans-
lates into measurable improvements in student
learning. We present a systematic analysis of
137 papers (2022–2025) across four sectors—
assessment and grammatical error correction,
feedback and writing support, content and
question generation, and tutoring and dia-
logue systems—introducing three formal gap
metrics: the Validity Gap (VG), measuring
the proportion of studies that omit learning-
outcome evaluation; the Deployment Readi-
ness Index (DRI), capturing real-world eval-
uation extent; and the Multilingual Equity
Index (MEI), quantifying language coverage.
We find VG = 0.927, DRI = 0.126, and
MEI = 0.126 field-wide. Within grammatical
error correction, no study demonstrates learn-
ing improvement (VG = 1.000). DRI corre-
lates positively with learning-outcome evalu-
ation (ρ = 0.425, p < 0.001), indicating
that real-world deployment and educational ac-
countability are structurally linked. Bengali,
Hindi, Urdu, Tamil, and Nepali—collectively
serving over 600 million school-age learners—
have no dedicated educational NLP system in
our corpus (MEI = 0.0). Our taxonomy, met-
rics, and five-gap research agenda constitute a
reproducible audit framework for educational
NLP accountability.
